Designing a home for Garmin ballistics
Timeline 21 months (2022 - 2023)
Project Role Lead UX Designer
Responsibilities Worked with a product manager, mobile software, and consumer electronics software to define requirements, identify opportunities for improvement, concept and design mockups, establish a handoff process, and create a plan for the app's future growth.

Garmin's Xero product family has been making waves in the hunting, archery, and rifle sports scene since its debut in 2018. I joined the Xero product team as they were working on a slate of exciting new products and their mobile counterparts.

Of these, the most highly anticipated was the C1 Pro radar chronograph. While the product team had full confidence in the device, its companion app ShotView needed a complete overhaul.

Background

Chronographs are devices that measure projectile speeds. They are often used by hunters and long-range shooters to optimize their gear for peak accuracy and consistency. Most higher-end chronographs come with apps that allow users to easily review, edit, and share their shot data.

While chronographs all basically do the same thing, the C1 Pro’s standout feature is its compact size and simple setup process.

Unlike its competitors at the time, the C1 Pro was small enough to fit in your pocket without compromising on accuracy or reliability.

We envisioned ShotView as the central hub for all future shooting and archery-related products at Garmin. But in the near-term, its main purpose would be to give shooters a way to view and manage shot data recorded on the C1 Pro.

Design

We started by designing all of the exciting interactions first. This came back to bite us when half of them had to be shelved so we could launch on time. The decision had been made fairly late into the project and required revisions to most of the app to account for the loss of key features, but gave us a roadmap for future updates and releases.

ShotView’s objectives pivoted to presenting shot stats in a way that would still be informative and insightful for users. The result is a minimalistic app with a strong emphasis on visual hierarchy to showcase session data.

ShotView’s Home and Session pages

A simplified map of ShotView's main screens is diagrammed below. The app's shallow, modular architecture serves as a scalable base so new features can easily be added in, whether for other ballistics devices or new chronograph features.

Ideally, users would pair the ShotView app and the C1 chronograph for the best possible experience, but there are some users who prefer to use the C1 on its own. To accommodate these users, I pulled in UI updates to mirror the app's design.

In some cases, this was challenging because of the device’s much smaller screen size and low resolution black-and-white display. Sample changes are displayed below.

These improvements help reduce cognitive load when the user is browsing saved sessions on the device.

There were a few new suggestions for improvements that were investigated and implemented after launch. One of them was the addition of bulk exports for sessions, which was a major perk for users who were recording lots of sessions and wanted to share them with other platforms for further analysis.

Conclusion

The C1 Pro and ShotView released together on October 19 2023 to great success, prompting the C1 to go out of stock for months afterward.

Garmin’s sales team had underestimated the units needed by about tenfold, eliciting this reaction from user @giumau1 on the Sniper’s Hide forum.

While external limitations prevented the team from implementing everything that had been planned, the need to adapt rapidly within those constraints helped me rethink the way I approach design projects. Serving as the UX lead on this product taught me to trust my intuition as a designer, and it was satisfying to see the tangible results of the whole team's contributions.

Overall, ShotView was a rare opportunity for me to design an app from the ground up and experience all of the ups and downs associated with the full process. I'm proud of have been a part of the project and am excited to see where it goes in the future.
